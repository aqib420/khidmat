Empowering the Differently Abled Persons Pakistan

An NGO fostering inclusivity for the blind, deaf and differently abled community in Karachi — through technology, training and counselling since 2008.

Join Our Mission

Who we are

A digital home for an 18-year mission

Operating from Ida Rieu College for Blind & Deaf in Karachi, Empowering the Differently Abled Persons Pakistan has, since 2008, bridged the gap for differently abled individuals through technology and stakeholder support. Led by founder Ms. Shazia Hasan, our work makes Pakistan more accessible — one learner, one family, one workplace at a time.

Computer Training

Specialized digital literacy using screen-reading software and adaptive technology for vocational success.

Learn More

Sign Language

Structured Pakistan Sign Language courses for the deaf and their families to bridge communication at home and work.

Learn More

Counselling

Professional support addressing the psychological adjustment and social challenges of living with disability.

Meet the founder

Ms. Shazia Hasan

Pakistan's first visually impaired Microsoft computer user. Educator, mentor and the founder of EDAP Pakistan — who lost her sight to glaucoma at age nine and has spent the decades since making sure that does not have to be the end of anyone's story.

"We are not disabled — we are differently abled."
