Our Programs

Three focused programs — built around the specific needs of the blind, deaf and differently abled community.

Vocational excellence

Computer Training for the Visually Impaired

We equip students with the skills needed to navigate modern workplaces using specialized screen-reading software and keyboard-driven workflows — the same tools used by visually impaired professionals around the world.

  • Screen readers — practical mastery of JAWS and NVDA.
  • Productivity — Microsoft Office and the web, navigated entirely by keyboard.
  • Job-ready skills — email, file management, document writing for the workplace.

Communication bridge

Sign Language & Social Agency

Pakistan Sign Language (PSL) training for deaf students and — just as importantly — for their parents, siblings and caregivers. Communication isn't a one-sided skill; the home must speak the language too.

  • PSL fundamentals — alphabet, numbers, everyday vocabulary.
  • Family classes — structured courses for parents and guardians.
  • Practical integration — confidence to communicate in classrooms, hospitals and workplaces.
Join Program

Mental well-being

Counselling & Family Support

Living with a disability — or loving someone who does — brings its own emotional weight. Our counselling is for both: the differently abled person and the family who often carries the challenge with them.

  • Individual sessions — psychological adjustment, self-esteem, confidence.
  • Family counselling — guidance for parents and guardians, who often need it more than they ask for it.
  • Social integration — practical support for workplace, school and community challenges.
