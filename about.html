About Our Mission

Bridging the gap for the differently abled community in Pakistan — since 2008.

Our story

Empowering since 2008

Empowering the Differently Abled Persons Pakistan was founded in 2008 with a clear vision: that physical or sensory disability should never stand in the way of professional excellence. The initiative is led by our founder and supervisor, Ms. Shazia Hasan — Pakistan's first visually impaired Microsoft computer user — whose lived experience shapes every program EDAP delivers.

Operating from the Ida Rieu College for Blind & Deaf in Karachi, Ms. Shazia oversees the project's core work — providing the specialized tools, vocational training and counselling the blind, deaf and differently abled community needs to lead independent, self-reliant lives.

Her guiding belief gives the organization its name and its spirit: we are not disabled — we are differently abled.

What drives us

Inclusion that lasts

Our work goes beyond classes. We equip families with the tools — sign language, counselling, adaptive computer skills — to support their loved ones every day. We believe inclusion doesn't end when a student leaves the campus; it begins there.

Every program is built around the people it serves: blind learners pursuing professional careers, deaf students bridging the world of hearing, and families finding their way through the emotional landscape of disability — together.
